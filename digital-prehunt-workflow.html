Digital Pre-Hunt Workflow

Screenshot or print this workflow for efficient app-based scouting before store visits.

Goal: Identify promising clearance items from home, then verify penny status in-store. This saves time and targets your hunt.

1 Open Home Depot App & Set Location

Actions:

  • Launch the Home Depot mobile app (iOS or Android)
  • Tap the location icon or menu to select your target store
  • Enable "Store Mode" if available to see real-time inventory
  • Make sure you're logged in for full features
Pro Tip: Set multiple stores as favorites so you can quickly switch between locations while scouting.

2 Search High-Probability Categories

Best Categories for Penny Items:

  • Hardware: Search "screws," "hooks," "brackets," "fasteners"
  • Electrical: Search "switches," "outlets," "wire connectors"
  • Lighting: Search "bulbs," "fixtures," "lamp parts"
  • Plumbing: Search "fittings," "connectors," "valves"
  • Paint: Search "brushes," "tape," "accessories"
  • Seasonal: Search "holiday," "clearance," "seasonal"
Pro Tip: Use generic search terms (e.g., "hooks") rather than brand names to see more results.

3 Apply Filters and Sort

Filter Strategy:

  1. Tap Filters or Sort at the top of search results
  2. Select "Clearance" or "Special Buy" if available
  3. Sort by "Price: Low to High"
  4. Set price range: $0.01 - $10.00 to focus on deeply discounted items
Pro Tip: Sometimes clearance items don't show up in filters. Try sorting by "Price: Low to High" without filters to catch everything under $5.

4 Identify Promising Items

Look for These Red Flags (Good Signs):

Indicator What It Means
Price ending in .03 or .02 Final markdown - likely to go penny soon
Price ending in .06 or .04 Second markdown - check back in 1-2 weeks
"Limited Stock" at your store Item is being phased out
"Out of Stock Online" May still be on floor but discontinued
Odd quantities (e.g., 3 in stock) Leftover inventory being cleared

5 Check Store Availability

For Each Promising Item:

  1. Tap the item to view details
  2. Scroll to "Check Nearby Stores" or "Store Availability"
  3. Look for your target store(s) in the list
  4. Note the quantity (1-5 units often means clearance)
  5. Check if multiple stores have it (widespread clearance = more likely penny)
Pro Tip: If an item shows "0" online but you saw it in-store recently, visit anyway. The system may not be updated.

6 Screenshot Critical Information

Capture These Details:

  • SKU number (located under product name or in details)
  • UPC barcode (if visible - saves time scanning in-store)
  • Current online price (to compare with in-store scan)
  • Store availability (which locations have stock)
  • Aisle/bay location (if shown - speeds up in-store hunt)

Organize Screenshots: Create a folder on your phone labeled "Penny Hunt [Date]" for each scouting session.

7 Build Your Target List

Create a Note or Document With:

Item Description SKU Online Price Store Aisle
Example: Wire Hooks 123456789 $1.03 Store #1234 Aisle 12
Light Switch 987654321 $0.42 Store #5678 Aisle 25
Paint Brush Set 456789123 $2.06 Store #1234 Aisle 8

Prioritize: Items ending in .03 or .02 go to the top of your list.

8 Plan Your Store Route

Route Optimization:

  1. Group items by aisle/department to minimize walking
  2. Start with the farthest section and work toward the exit
  3. Check clearance endcaps along the way
  4. Save overhead and locked cases for last (they require more time)

Timing: Visit within 24-48 hours of scouting. Penny items move fast.

9 In-Store Verification

When You Arrive at the Store:

  1. Navigate to each target item using your screenshots
  2. Scan the UPC barcode at self-checkout (or use the app)
  3. If it rings up $0.01, add to cart immediately
  4. If it's still at the online price, leave it (not penny yet)
  5. If it's missing, check nearby aisles or ask (discreetly)
Pro Tip: Don't be discouraged if only 1 out of 10 items is actually penny. That's normal. You're playing the numbers game.

10 Track and Refine

After Your Visit:

  • Note which categories had the most penny hits
  • Track how long items stayed at .03/.02 before going penny
  • Identify which stores have slower clearance processes (better for hunting)
  • Share successful SKUs with the community (with dates and locations)

Improve Your Process: Over time, you'll learn which search terms, categories, and stores yield the best results.

Quick Reference: App Scouting Dos and Don'ts

DO DON'T
Search broad categories (e.g., "hooks," "switches") Search specific brands (limits results)
Check multiple stores in one scouting session Only scout one store (you'll miss opportunities)
Screenshot SKUs and prices for reference Rely on memory (you'll forget details)
Visit stores within 24-48 hours Wait a week (items will be pulled or sold)
Track patterns over multiple hunts Expect immediate results (this takes practice)

Advanced App Scouting Techniques

1. The "Out of Stock" Strategy

Items showing "Out of Stock" online but "Limited Stock" in-store are often penny candidates. The system has written them off but physical inventory remains.

2. The "Compare Stores" Method

If the same SKU is clearance at 3+ stores in your area, it's likely a regional markdown that will go penny soon.

3. The "Reverse Search" Trick

If you find a penny item in-store, search for related items in the same brand or category on the app. They may be on the same markdown cadence.

4. The "Notification Watch"

Some communities share SKUs of items about to go penny. Add these to your app favorites and check daily.

How to Use This Workflow

  • Print and keep in your car: Reference while parked before entering stores
  • Screenshot for mobile reference: Pull up on your phone while scouting
  • Practice the workflow 3-5 times: It becomes second nature quickly
  • Customize for your stores: Add notes about which categories work best locally
