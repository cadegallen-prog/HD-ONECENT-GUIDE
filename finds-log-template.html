Column Definitions

  • Date: When you found the penny item
  • Store #: Home Depot store number for reference
  • Item Description: Brief name of the product
  • SKU/UPC: Product identification number
  • Category: Hardware, Electrical, Lighting, Plumbing, etc.
  • Qty: How many units you purchased
  • Retail Price: Original price before markdowns
  • Paid: What you actually paid (usually $0.01 per item)
  • Resale Price: What you sold it for (if reselling)
  • Platform: Where you sold it (eBay, Mercari, Facebook, etc.)
  • Status: Keeping, Listed, Sold, etc.

How to Use This Log

  • For Personal Use: Track savings and justify the time spent hunting
  • For Resellers: Calculate profit margins and track inventory
  • For Tax Purposes: Document cost of goods sold if reselling
  • For Analysis: Identify which categories and stores are most profitable

Tips for Effective Tracking

  • Log immediately: Fill in details right after purchase (while receipt is fresh)
  • Keep receipts: Attach or file receipts with your log for proof
  • Update resale info: Come back and fill in when items sell
  • Review monthly: Calculate ROI and adjust your hunting strategy
  • Share SKUs: Help the community by posting successful finds online

Reseller-Specific Tips

  • Calculate fees: Subtract platform fees (eBay ~13%, Mercari ~13%, etc.) from resale price
  • Track shipping costs: Include shipping expenses in your profit calculations
  • Note listing time: How long did it take to sell? Factor in storage costs
  • Identify winners: Which categories sell fastest and for highest profit?
  • Avoid duds: Stop buying categories that don't sell or take too long

Digital Alternative

Consider creating a spreadsheet version with formulas for automatic calculations:

  • Total Savings: =SUM(Retail Price - Paid)
  • Total Revenue: =SUM(Resale Price)
  • Net Profit: =SUM(Resale Price - Paid - Fees - Shipping)
  • ROI: =(Net Profit / Total Cost) * 100
