Quick Start Guide: Your First 5 Steps

On This Page

New to penny hunting? This guide will get you from zero to your first successful hunt in 5 clear steps.

Important: Penny hunting requires patience, practice, and the right expectations. This isn't a guaranteed money-makerâ€”it's a skill that develops over time.

Step 1: Understand What You're Getting Into (15 minutes)

Before you visit a single store, understand the fundamentals:

Read These Pages First:

  1. What Are Penny Items? - Learn what penny items are (and aren't)
  2. Clearance Lifecycle - Understand how items reach $0.01
  3. Facts vs. Myths - Separate reality from wishful thinking

Key Concepts to Grasp:

  • Penny items are not meant to be soldâ€”they're marked for removal
  • Success depends on timing, luck, and discretion
  • Stores can refuse the sale at any time
  • Most trips will yield nothingâ€”that's normal

Step 2: Set Realistic Expectations (5 minutes)

Before you invest time and gas money, be honest with yourself:

This Works For You If:

  • You have time to hunt regularly (weekly or more)
  • You're okay with empty trips and dry spells
  • You live near multiple Home Depot locations
  • You can stay low-key and respectful in stores
  • You're interested in hardware, tools, or reselling

This Probably Won't Work If:

  • You need guaranteed savings every trip
  • You're looking for a get-rich-quick scheme
  • You can't handle confrontation or rejection
  • You only have one nearby store
  • You expect to quit your job from penny finds

Reality Check: Most experienced hunters report finding usable penny items 1â€“3 times per month, with many empty trips in between. If that sounds discouraging, this might not be for you.

Step 3: Prepare for Your First Hunt (30 minutes)

Get ready before you step foot in a store:

Digital Preparation:

  • Download the Home Depot app (iOS or Android)
  • Create a free account (or sign in)
  • Enable location services so the app shows your local stores
  • Join a penny shopping community (Facebook, Reddit, Discord) to get real-time tips

What to Bring:

  • Your smartphone (for scanning barcodes and checking prices)
  • A small bag or basket (to carry items discreetly)
  • Payment method (debit/credit card works best at self-checkout)
  • Printed cheat sheet (optional - download from Resources)

Mental Preparation:

  • Plan to spend 45â€“90 minutes on your first hunt
  • Expect to find nothing and be pleasantly surprised if you do
  • Know that you might be denied at checkoutâ€”it happens
  • Stay calm, polite, and low-key no matter what

Step 4: Execute Your First Store Visit (60-90 minutes)

Follow this step-by-step process on your first hunt:

A. Scout with the App (Before You Go)

  1. Open the Home Depot app
  2. Search for categories like "hardware," "lighting," or "electrical"
  3. Filter by "Clearance" or sort by "Price: Low to High"
  4. Look for items under $5 with weird pricing (.03, .02, .06, .04)
  5. Check "Store Availability" at your target location
  6. Screenshot the SKU and barcode for in-store scanning

B. Hunt the Store (In-Person)

  1. Start with clearance endcaps near the main aisles
  2. Check price tags for endings in .03, .02, .06, or .04
  3. Look for tag dates that are 2+ weeks old
  4. Scan items at self-checkout (or with the app) to check prices
  5. Focus on small, easy-to-carry items for your first hunt
  6. Stay in high-clearance sections: Hardware, Electrical, Plumbing, Paint, Seasonal

C. Test Checkout (If You Find Something)

  1. Go to self-checkout (not staffed registers)
  2. Scan the manufacturer's UPC barcode (not the clearance tag)
  3. If it rings up at $0.01, pay immediately
  4. Print your receipt and keep it
  5. Leave quietlyâ€”don't celebrate or brag

Pro Tip: On your first trip, focus on learning the store layout and understanding the clearance system more than finding penny items. Familiarity pays off on future visits.

Step 5: Learn and Improve (Ongoing)

After your first hunt (whether successful or not), level up your skills:

First Week Goals:

  • Visit 2â€“3 different stores to compare clearance inventory
  • Note which stores have more clearance stock and less-trained staff
  • Join online communities and observe how experienced hunters share info
  • Read the full guides on Digital Pre-Hunt and In-Store Strategy

First Month Goals:

  • Track your visits using the Store Notes Template
  • Learn the clearance cadence for your local stores (Cadence A vs. Cadence B)
  • Identify your best hunting times (early morning, late evening, weekdays)
  • Build rapport by being polite and low-key every visit
  • Find 1â€“3 penny items (even if small or low-value)

Beyond the First Month:

  • Expand to more stores as you get comfortable
  • Learn store-specific patterns (which sections get marked down most)
  • Network with other hunters to share finds and tips
  • Refine your checkout strategy based on what works at each location
  • Consider reselling if you're finding items with resale value

Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why It's Bad What to Do Instead
Asking staff for "penny items" They'll pull them immediately Scout quietly and verify prices yourself
Scanning clearance tags Freezes the terminal and flags staff Always scan the UPC barcode
Going to the same store daily You'll get recognized and watched Rotate between 3+ stores
Buying items you don't want Wastes money and time Only buy items you can use or resell
Arguing with employees Gets you banned and ruins it for others Stay polite and walk away if denied
Giving up after one empty trip Penny hunting requires persistence Plan for 5+ trips before judging success
