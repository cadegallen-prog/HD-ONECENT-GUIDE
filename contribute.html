Contribute to the Community

On This Page

This guide exists because of community knowledge sharing. Here's how you can contribute and help others succeed.

Why Community Contributions Matter

Penny hunting thrives on shared knowledge. When experienced hunters share what they've learned, everyone benefits:

  • Beginners learn faster and avoid costly mistakes
  • Experienced hunters discover new tactics and regional differences
  • The community grows stronger and more resilient
  • Store patterns become clearer through collective observation

What You Can Contribute

1. Success Stories

Share your wins (and losses) to help others learn from your experience.

What to Include:

  • Item details: SKU, UPC, category (e.g., "hardware," "lighting")
  • Store info: City, state (no specific addresses)
  • Date found: Helps others understand timing
  • Price tag details: Ending (.03, .02, etc.) and markdown date
  • Checkout method: Self-checkout, staffed register, challenges faced
  • Lessons learned: What worked, what didn't

Template for Success Stories:

Item Found: [Description]
SKU/UPC: [Numbers]
Location: [City, State]
Date: [Date found]
Price Tag: [Ending and markdown date]
Checkout: [Method used]
What I Learned: [Key takeaways]

2. Tips from Experienced Hunters

Share tactics, strategies, and insights you've developed over time.

Valuable Tips Include:

  • Store-specific patterns: "My local stores mark down lighting on Thursdays"
  • Timing observations: "Early morning = fewer staff interruptions"
  • Regional differences: "Stores in [region] seem to use Cadence B more often"
  • Category insights: "Electrical items rarely get pulled before hitting penny"
  • Checkout tactics: "Bringing a 'keeper' item helps at self-checkout"

3. Store Policy Updates

Home Depot policies change. If you notice new enforcement patterns, share them.

Examples:

  • "Store in [city] started checking self-checkout screens more closely"
  • "Manager at [location] now requires override for all $0.01 transactions"
  • "New clearance removal policy: items pulled within 24 hours of going penny"

4. Clearance Cycle Observations

Track and share markdown patterns to help others predict when items will go penny.

Useful Data:

  • Cadence timelines: How long items stay at each price ending
  • Markdown days: Which days of the week markdowns happen
  • Category-specific cycles: Seasonal vs. hardware vs. lighting
  • Regional variations: Differences between states or regions

5. Community Resources

Create and share tools, templates, or guides that help other hunters.

Ideas:

  • Custom tracking spreadsheets
  • Regional store lists with notes
  • Screenshot guides for using the app
  • Printable cheat sheets with local cadence info

How to Share Your Knowledge

Online Communities

Join and contribute to penny shopping groups:

  • Facebook Groups: Search for "Home Depot penny shopping" or "clearance hunting"
  • Reddit: r/Flipping, r/HomeDepot, or deal-focused subreddits
  • Discord Servers: Many reselling communities have penny shopping channels
  • Reseller Forums: Share finds and strategies with other resellers

Contribution Guidelines

When sharing in communities, follow these best practices:

DO:

  • Be accurate: Share only confirmed information, not rumors
  • Include details: SKUs, dates, and specifics help others verify
  • Be helpful: Answer beginner questions patiently
  • Give credit: Acknowledge where you learned tactics or strategies
  • Update old posts: If a find is no longer valid, edit your post

DON'T:

  • Exaggerate: Don't inflate values or success rates
  • Post rumors: "I heard X store has pennies" wastes everyone's time
  • Share staff names/faces: Respect employee privacy
  • Post store addresses: City and state is enough
  • Brag excessively: Share to help, not to show off

Code of Conduct for Contributors

To keep the community strong and welcoming:

Respect and Inclusivity

  • Welcome beginners: Everyone starts somewhere
  • No gatekeeping: Share knowledge freely
  • Be patient: Answer repeated questions politely
  • Celebrate others' wins: Community success benefits everyone

Honesty and Transparency

  • Admit mistakes: If your info was wrong, correct it
  • Share failures too: Dry spells and denials are part of the process
  • Set realistic expectations: Don't oversell the potential earnings

Responsibility and Ethics

  • Follow store policies: Don't encourage rule-breaking
  • Respect employees: They're just doing their jobs
  • Avoid heat: Don't share tactics that draw excessive attention
  • Think long-term: Actions that burn stores hurt everyone
