What Are Penny Items?

On This Page

4 min read Beginner

Page Summary

At Home Depot, a "penny item" refers to merchandise that rings up at $0.01 due to internal inventory clearance systemsâ€”not public promotions. This page explains what penny items really are, how the ZMA system works, and why these items exist in the first place.

These Aren't Sales

Let's be clear: penny items are not intentional deals or customer-facing discounts.

They're items that:

  • Have been chosen for removal
  • Are no longer intended for sale
  • Still happen to be on shelves because of oversight or delays

If you find one and successfully buy it, it's because something slipped through the cracks in Home Depot's inventory management process.

How It Works: Zero Margin Adjustment (ZMA)

The $0.01 price is triggered by a financial mechanism called Zero Margin Adjustment (ZMA).

The ZMA Process
  1. Item marked for removal
    Home Depot decides an item is no longer worth selling (discontinued, damaged, seasonal changeover, etc.)
  2. System applies ZMA
    The system uses ZMA to reduce the item's value to nearly zeroâ€”specifically, $0.01
  3. Flagged for removal
    The item is flagged for removal from the sales floor
  4. Staff pulls item
    Staff are supposed to pull it and dispose of it or liquidate it
But Sometimes...
  • Staff miss it during their clearance sweep
  • There's a backlog in pulling items
  • The item gets shoved to a back shelf or misplaced

That's when shoppers find it.

Why Do Penny Items Exist?

Penny items exist because retail operations aren't perfect. Even with advanced inventory systems and handheld scanners, items slip through.

Common reasons include:

  • High volume â€” Stores process thousands of SKUs, and some get overlooked
  • Staff turnover â€” Not every employee knows the clearance process inside and out
  • Seasonal chaos â€” Transitions between seasons create backlogs
  • Misplaced inventory â€” Items get moved by customers or restocking errors
  • Overhead storage â€” Items stored on top shelves are harder to track and remove

What Penny Items Are NOT

Let's clear up some misconceptions:

  • NOT a secret sale â€” Home Depot isn't running a promotion
  • NOT legal gray area â€” Buying one isn't illegal, but stores can refuse the sale
  • NOT guaranteed â€” Just because something hit $0.01 doesn't mean it's still there
  • NOT consistent â€” What works at one store may not work at another
  • NOT risk-free â€” Staff can (and often do) deny these sales

Bottom Line

Penny items are the last stage of Home Depot's clearance cycle. They're meant to be removed, not sold.
