Digital Pre-Hunt: Using the Home Depot App & Website

Before heading into a store, use Home Depot's app or website to scout items that might have reached penny status.

Important: The app won't show you the $0.01 price directly—but it can give you signals that an item has been marked internally.

Step 1: Set Your Store Location

In the app or online, set your specific store location before searching.

Why this matters:

  • Inventory and pricing data is store-specific
  • Wrong location = wrong info
  • An item might be pennied at Store A but still full price at Store B

How to set it:

  • Open the Home Depot app or website
  • Tap the location icon or "My Store" dropdown
  • Select the exact store you plan to visit

Step 2: Search by SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)

Find the SKU number on:

  • Product packaging
  • Yellow clearance tag
  • Community posts (if someone shared a known penny item)

Use that number in the Home Depot app or site search bar.

Pro Tip: Searching by SKU is more accurate than searching by product name, which can pull up similar but unrelated items.

Step 3: Interpret What You See

Once you've searched an item, use the chart below to decode what the listing might mean:

Online Status What It Could Mean
In Stock + Clearance Price visible Still in clearance cycle, not pennied yet
In Stock + Full Price Still active inventory
Out of Stock / Unavailable / Ship to Store Only + Full Price Strong penny candidate — system may have pennied it, but it hasn't been removed from shelves yet
Clearance price still showing online Not yet a penny item

Key Insight: If the system shows a full price but no stock, it might have already hit $0.01 internally and just hasn't been pulled.

Limitations to Keep in Mind

Digital scouting is helpful, but it's not foolproof.

  • Online data isn't real-time — there can be a delay of 1–2 days
  • The penny price ($0.01) never shows online
  • You still need in-store confirmation to be sure — this is just a filtering step

Don't make a special trip based solely on app data. Use it to narrow down which stores and items are worth checking in person.

When to Go In-Store

Only go check in person if:

  • You've found an item that shows no local stock but is still listed online
  • You're tracking the item based on its clearance cycle and tag date
  • You've seen reports in penny shopping communities about that item pennying out recently

If the item shows in stock at full clearance price, it's probably not pennied yet. Wait and check again in a week or two.

Advanced Tip: Track Multiple Stores

If you live near multiple Home Depot locations, check the app for the same SKU across all of them.

Why? Because:

  • One store might penny an item while another still has it at full price
  • Store-specific returns or overstock can trigger penny status at one location only
  • Seasonal changeovers happen at different speeds per store

Switch your "My Store" location in the app and search again. Compare inventory status across stores to find the best candidate.
