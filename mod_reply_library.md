# Moderator Reply Library

A collection of reusable moderator replies for the Home Depot Penny Items Facebook group.

---

## Non-Penny Items

### Reply 1: Regular Clearance Item Posted
**Situation:** Someone posts an item that's just regular clearance, not a penny item.

**Reply:**
Hi! This looks like a regular clearance item rather than a penny find. Penny items ring up as $0.01 at the register and are typically unmarked on the shelf. Regular clearance items (even deep discounts) don't qualify as penny posts for this group. Thanks for understanding!

### Reply 2: Misunderstanding Price Tags
**Situation:** Posted item clearly shows a non-penny price tag.

**Reply:**
Thanks for sharing, but this item is marked at [price] which means it's not a penny item. Penny items ring up as exactly $0.01 at the register and usually have no price tag or show the original price. Feel free to share actual penny finds!

### Reply 3: Wishful Thinking Post
**Situation:** Someone posts "Do you think this will go to a penny?"

**Reply:**
We appreciate your enthusiasm, but this group is for confirmed penny finds only, not speculation about what might go to a penny in the future. If you find this item rings up as $0.01, please come back and share it with a receipt showing the penny price!

## "Is This Still a Penny?" Posts

### Reply 4: Basic YMMV Explanation
**Situation:** Standard "Is this still a penny at your store?" question.

**Reply:**
Penny items are YMMV (Your Mileage May Vary). What's a penny at one store may be full price at another, or already gone. The best way to know is to check your local store! Inventory and clearance cycles vary widely by location.

### Reply 5: Item From Weeks/Months Ago
**Situation:** Asking about an old penny find.

**Reply:**
This post is from [date], so it's unlikely these are still available as pennies. Penny items typically get pulled from shelves within days to weeks. Your best bet is to check current posts and visit your local store to see what's currently on clearance!

### Reply 6: Expecting Guarantees
**Situation:** Someone wants a definite yes/no answer.

**Reply:**
There's no way to know if this is still a penny without checking your specific store. Every Home Depot location has different inventory and clearance schedules. The only way to find out is to visit your store or call and ask if they have SKU [number] in stock!

## Calling Stores

### Reply 7: Planning to Call About Penny Items
**Situation:** Someone announces they're going to call the store about a penny item.

**Reply:**
Please don't call stores asking if items are "pennied out" or $0.01. Employees aren't allowed to hold penny items, and these calls create problems for staff who are just doing their job. The best approach is to visit in person and check for yourself!

### Reply 8: Store Said They Don't Do Pennies
**Situation:** Someone reports calling and being told "we don't have penny items."

**Reply:**
Home Depot employees often aren't aware of the penny system or aren't permitted to discuss it over the phone. Penny items aren't advertised deals—they're inventory management markers. Visiting in person is always more effective than calling!

### Reply 9: Asking for Store Holds
**Situation:** Someone wants to call and have an item held.

**Reply:**
Home Depot policy prohibits holding penny items. These are clearance items marked for disposal, not regular merchandise. Please don't ask employees to break policy—it puts them in an uncomfortable position and could affect their job. First come, first served!

## Being Rude to Employees

### Reply 10: Demanding or Entitled Behavior
**Situation:** Someone describes being aggressive with staff.

**Reply:**
This behavior isn't acceptable and doesn't represent our group. Home Depot employees are doing their jobs—they don't control penny pricing and aren't hiding items from you. Treating staff with respect is non-negotiable. Future posts promoting rude behavior will result in removal from the group.

### Reply 11: Arguing About Prices
**Situation:** Someone brags about arguing with cashiers about penny prices.

**Reply:**
If an item doesn't ring up as a penny, it's not a penny at that location. Arguing with cashiers or managers won't change the price in the system and creates unnecessary conflict. Please be respectful of store staff—they don't set the prices.

### Reply 12: "The Employee Was Useless"
**Situation:** Someone complains that an employee couldn't help them find penny items.

**Reply:**
Employees aren't trained to help customers find penny items—that's not part of their job. They're there to assist with regular merchandise and home improvement questions. Finding pennies is a treasure hunt we do on our own. Please be kind to staff!

## Empty Shelves / Dry Runs

### Reply 13: Complaining About Finding Nothing
**Situation:** Long rant about wasting time with no finds.

**Reply:**
Penny hunting requires patience and isn't guaranteed. Some trips you'll find amazing deals, other times you'll find nothing—that's the nature of clearance hunting. The group shares finds to help each other, but we can't control what's available at your specific store!

### Reply 14: "This Group is Useless"
**Situation:** Frustrated post saying the group doesn't help.

**Reply:**
This group shares confirmed penny finds to help members know what to look for. However, YMMV means inventory varies by location. We're a community of treasure hunters sharing tips, not a guarantee service. If you're feeling frustrated, taking a break from the hunt might help!

### Reply 15: Blaming the Group for Inventory
**Situation:** Someone accuses members of clearing out stores.

**Reply:**
We don't control Home Depot's inventory or clearance cycles. Items go to penny status because they're discontinued or seasonal—not because of this group. Many factors affect whether items are in stock at your location. Let's keep the focus positive and celebrate finds when they happen!

### Reply 16: Multiple Dry Run Complaints
**Situation:** Someone posts repeatedly about finding nothing.

**Reply:**
We understand the frustration, but penny hunting is hit-or-miss by nature. Some members check stores for weeks before finding anything. If it's not fun anymore, it's okay to take a break! When you do find something, the thrill makes it worthwhile.

## General Group Rules

### Reply 22: No Receipt/Proof Provided
**Situation:** Penny claim without verification.

**Reply:**
Please include a receipt or photo showing the $0.01 price to verify penny finds. This helps prevent confusion and ensures the group information is accurate. Feel free to repost with proof!

### Reply 23: Low-Quality or Blurry Photos
**Situation:** Can't identify the item from the photo.

**Reply:**
Could you share a clearer photo or include the SKU number? Clear images help other members identify items at their local stores. Thanks!

### Reply 24: Off-Topic Post
**Situation:** Post not related to Home Depot penny items.

**Reply:**
This group is specifically for Home Depot penny finds. For deals from other stores, please check out the appropriate groups. Keeping posts on-topic helps everyone find the information they need!

### Reply 25: Duplicate Post
**Situation:** Same item posted multiple times recently.

**Reply:**
This item was shared earlier today/this week. Please check recent posts before submitting to avoid duplicates. Thanks for understanding!

## Questions About How It Works

### Reply 26: "What Does Penny Mean?"
**Situation:** New member asking basic questions.

**Reply:**
Welcome! A "penny item" rings up as exactly $0.01 at the register. These are discontinued or seasonal items Home Depot is clearing out. Check the pinned post/group guide for tips on finding them. Happy hunting!

### Reply 27: "How Do I Find Penny Items?"
**Situation:** Asking for search strategies.

**Reply:**
Finding penny items takes time and patience! Look for clearance sections, end caps, and unmarked items in seasonal areas. Use the Home Depot app to check prices by scanning barcodes. Check the group's guide for detailed tips. Good luck!

### Reply 28: "Can I Return Penny Items?"
**Situation:** Questions about returns.

**Reply:**
Penny items can technically be returned per Home Depot policy, but it's generally discouraged since you only paid $0.01. If the item is defective, you can try, but results vary by store. When in doubt, remember you only risked a penny!
